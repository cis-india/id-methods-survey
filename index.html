Identification of Gender and Sexuality of Subjects in Big Data Sets

Data abundance is now has become the norm. With the proliferation of digital platforms, content generated by or from users has grown exponentially and with it, a growing recognition of the uses of such large data sets to provide insight into complex real-world problems. Demographic inference -- the prediction of population characteristics, such as gender, age, or geography -- from big data sources is emerging as a significant component ofto big data systems, with thewithhaving the promise of being used potential to be used to inform decision making in a variety of sectors and industries. everything from marketing, law to development decisions.

The detection of a personâ€™s gender and/or sexuality is often a key and foundational component of demographic inference. This survey has been conducted with the purpose of providing an approachable overview of the various methods researchers and other actors are using to infer gender and sexuality from large data sets. They are categorized according to two aspects of the methods surveyed: (1) the specific classifier utilized to infer gender, and (2) the data source being analyzed for said inference.
